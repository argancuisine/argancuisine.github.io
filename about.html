About Us

Where Argan comes from?

Argan is an evergreen Moroccan tree or shrub which has hard, heavy wood and yields seeds whose oil is used in cosmetics and cooking.
The scientific name argania is derived from argan, the name of the tree in Tamazight, the Tamazight language which is spoken by the native people of Morocco and the majority of the people living in the areas where the tree is endemic. Tamazight has a rich vocabulary for the various parts of the fruit, its stages of ripeness, and its harvesting and processing.
What we are:

Argan Food Truck is a Melbourne based mobile catering service which specializes in Moroccan food trading throughout Vitoria, mainly at festivals and markets.

We are a  family owned business operating a mobile food van, trying to live out our dream of bringing the unique Moroccan cuisine we grew up with straight to Melbourne.
As local musician, Brahim Benhim, leader of "La Kasbah" Moroccan Ensemble put it: "Abderrahman was one of those knowledge and experiences Moroccan tour leaders who would make sure every single tourist was OK and particularly with those that had special dietary requirements. He had spent days and nights of camel treks with tourists into the Sahara Desert tucking them into a tasty Moroccan Tagine and lots more fun, including being comic. He's been telling me about his aspiration to create a food business for himself and now I'm so happy for him that "Argan Moroccan Street Food" was recently created. First mobile Moroccan canteen ever, an extraordinary invention designed and put together by the man himself".

All our traditional recipes have been handed down from generations landing on your plate. Our motto is '' perfection " which we expect the customer to notice when tasting our food, because we believe in quality and not quantity. As we are environmentally friendly, all our utensils are biodegradable.
